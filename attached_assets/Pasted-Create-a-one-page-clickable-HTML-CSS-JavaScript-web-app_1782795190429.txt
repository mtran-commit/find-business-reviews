Create a one-page clickable HTML/CSS/JavaScript web app called “Compare Reviews”.

Purpose:
The user enters a business name and the app compares that business’s public review ratings across Google, TripAdvisor, Yelp and Facebook.

Design style:
Use a premium black-and-white design with dark navy blue accents.
Do not use orange or purple gradients.
Keep the app clean, modern, mobile responsive and professional.

Colour palette:
- Background: #F7F7F4
- Card background: #FFFFFF
- Primary text: #050505
- Secondary text: #5F6368
- Border: #E5E5E5
- Black: #000000
- Dark navy: #071A3D
- Navy hover: #0B2A5B
- Soft navy background: #EEF3FF
- Light grey button: #F1F1F1

Important visual rules:
- Business logo should appear in full colour if available.
- Business photos/images should remain in full colour.
- Review platform icons should remain in their brand colours:
  - Google: #4285F4
  - TripAdvisor: #00AA6C
  - Yelp: #D32323
  - Facebook: #1877F2
- All generic app icons should be black and white.
- Stars should be black, not yellow.
- Progress bars should be dark navy.

Build this as a single index.html file with inline CSS and JavaScript.

6. Insight grid
   Add cards for:
   - Rating Gap Alert
   - Review Summary
   - Best For
   - Red Flags
   - Reputation Trend
   - Review Pay Verified

Rating Gap Alert logic:
- If the highest rating minus lowest rating is 0.9 or more, show warning:
  “Warning: ratings vary strongly between platforms. Check reviews carefully before booking or buying.”
- If gap is 0.5 or more, show:
  “Moderate rating gap detected. Customer experience may vary depending on the platform.”
- Otherwise show:
  “Ratings are consistent across platforms. This is a positive trust signal.”

Review Summary logic:
- If Trust Score >= 85 and gap < 0.5:
  “Customers appear to have a strong and consistent experience across multiple review platforms. The business shows a high rating average, good review volume and low rating gap.”
- If Trust Score >= 70:
  “The business appears generally trusted, but there are some differences between platforms. Users should read recent reviews before making a decision.”
- Otherwise:
  “The business may have mixed customer feedback. Ratings are either lower, inconsistent, or supported by limited review volume.”

Best For tags:
If average rating >= 4.5 show:
- Customer Service
- Trusted Choice
- Premium Experience
- Highly Rated

Otherwise show:
- Good Value
- Worth Comparing
- Check Recent Reviews

Red Flags:
Show:
- Low review volume if total reviews are under 300
- Large rating gap if gap is 0.7 or above
- Lower average rating if average rating is below 4.0
If no red flags, show:
- No major red flags found

Reputation Trend:
If Trust Score >= 85 and gap < 0.5:
- Text: “The reputation trend looks strong. Ratings are high and consistent across multiple platforms.”
- Pill: “Strong Reputation”

If Trust Score >= 70:
- Text: “The reputation trend looks stable, but users should check recent reviews before deciding.”
- Pill: “Stable”

Otherwise:
- Text: “The reputation trend may need attention. Ratings show mixed or weaker signals.”
- Pill: “Check Carefully”

Review Pay Verified:
Show:
“Review Pay verified customer data can be connected later to show whether real transaction-based reviews are available.”
Pill:
“Coming Soon”

Functionality:
- The app must be fully clickable.
- Pressing Enter in the search box should trigger search.
- Quick search chips should trigger search.
- Nearby cards should trigger search.
- Visit Website should open the business website in a new tab.
- Call should use tel: link.
- Directions should open Google Maps in a new tab.
- Save Comparison should show an alert:
  “Comparison saved. Your developer can connect this to your database.”
- New Search resets the interface.
