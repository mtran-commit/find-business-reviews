Improve the paid AI Customer Review Sentiment Report template using the following final upgrades.

The current report is good, but it needs to feel more valuable, more executive-focused, and safer from a legal/data accuracy point of view.

Keep the current structure and content, but add these improvements.

1. CHANGE REPORT BADGE WORDING

Replace the badge text:

PAID REPORT

with:

Premium AI Reputation Report

or:

Premium Business Intelligence Report

This makes the report feel more valuable and professional.

2. ADD IMPORTANT NOTICE ON PAGE 1

Add a small disclaimer/notice directly under the KPI cards on page 1.

Text:

Important Notice:
This report is generated using available public review data and AI analysis. AI may make mistakes, and public data may be incomplete, outdated or unavailable. This report is provided for general business insight only and does not guarantee improved ratings, sales, bookings, rankings or revenue.

Style:
- light grey card
- small text
- rounded corners
- not too dominant
- visible on page 1

3. ADD DATA CUT-OFF NOTICE

Add a “Data Cut-Off” notice on page 1 near the generated date or under the important notice.

Text:

Data Cut-Off:
This report reflects available public review data at or around the generated date. Ratings, review counts, review text, business information and competitor data may change after this report is produced.

This should also appear near the final disclaimer at the end of the report.

4. ADD CUSTOMER VOICE SUMMARY NEAR THE FRONT

Add a new section on page 1 or early page 2 called:

Customer Voice Summary

This should summarise in 3–5 bullet points what customers appear to think about the business.

Example format:

Customer Voice Summary:
- Customers most often praise: [top positive themes]
- Customers repeatedly mention: [review tags / common words]
- Main improvement signals: [top concerns]
- Fastest trust opportunity: [one quick action]
- What to monitor next: [main risk area]

For example, if the report has tags like bakery, cafe, pharmacy, food court and fresh vegetables, explain what those tags mean in plain business language.

5. ADD TOP 3 ACTIONS THIS WEEK ON PAGE 1

Add a new highlighted box on page 1 or early page 2 called:

Top 3 Actions This Week

This must be very practical.

Example:

Top 3 Actions This Week:
1. Audit stock gaps for high-demand items.
2. Respond publicly to recent service or safety feedback.
3. Add QR review prompts at checkout, receipts or follow-up messages.

These actions should be generated based on the actual review analysis, not hardcoded.

6. ADD “HOW TO USE THIS REPORT” SECTION

Add a small section near the beginning:

How to Use This Report

Text:

Use this report to understand what customers appear to value, what may be creating hesitation, and which reputation actions should be prioritised. This report should be reviewed together with your own business records, staff feedback and customer service data.

7. ADD CONFIDENCE LEVELS TO INSIGHTS

For each major insight, add a confidence level:

Confidence: High
Confidence: Medium
Confidence: Low

Use this logic:
- High confidence: supported by high review volume, repeated review tags, repeated themes or many review snippets.
- Medium confidence: supported by some review text, limited snippets or one platform only.
- Low confidence: inferred from limited data, missing platforms or small review sample.

Example:

Bakery strength
Confidence: High
Based on 94 customer topic mentions.

Safety/service concern
Confidence: Medium
Based on limited available negative review text.

Do not pretend confidence is high if data is limited.

Competitor Note:

Service/safety feedback
